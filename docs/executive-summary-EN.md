# Executive Summary — Néovolt Grid+

**Prepared for:** Néovolt Steering Committee · **By:** cross-functional project team · **Scope:** one-week framing & prototyping sprint

## Context

Néovolt, a regional energy distributor serving ~600,000 delivery points, operates **critical infrastructure** but under-exploits the flood of data generated by its smart meters: siloed databases, uneven data quality, limited forecasting capabilities, no decision dashboards, an un-audited security posture, and no overall governance.

The **Néovolt Grid+** programme aims to address these challenges. We were asked not to build a production-ready system, but to deliver a **credible prototype and a decision-support package**.

## What we delivered

A working, end-to-end prototype covering the full data chain, together with the management and governance framework required to support future deployment.

* **Data platform (engineering):** an ingestion-and-quality pipeline transforming raw meter readings into clean and usable datasets (**98.5% usable data** after cleaning and validation), a relational warehouse, a **REST API**, and Docker-based deployment.
* **Consumption forecasting (data science):** development and comparison of several Machine Learning models using historical consumption and weather data. The selected model achieves a **MAPE below 10%**, providing useful forecasts for anticipating demand peaks and supporting energy planning.
* **Decision analytics:** interactive dashboards for network operations, finance and customer-relations teams, together with text mining of 3,000 customer complaints (average satisfaction **2.45/5**, with billing identified as the main source of dissatisfaction).
* **Security & compliance:** SIEM analysis revealing a **real attack scenario** in the logs, an EBIOS risk assessment, a DevSecOps dependency audit (3 vulnerabilities identified and fixed), and an NIS 2 incident-response runbook.
* **Management & governance:** a complete business case, project roadmap, governance framework, RACI matrix, change-management plan and project steering dashboard.

## Financial outlook

The programme is estimated at **€360k** with annual operating costs of approximately **€75k**.

Expected annual benefits are estimated at **€291k**, resulting in an annual net benefit of approximately **€216k**.

The estimated **return on investment is around 20 months**, making the initiative financially attractive while remaining realistic and aligned with the project's scope.

## Key recommendation

**Build the data platform and consumption forecasting capabilities first.**

Consumption forecasting delivers immediate operational value by helping Néovolt anticipate demand peaks, improve planning and support energy purchasing decisions.

Detection of anomalies and fraud remains a relevant future evolution that can leverage the same data platform once governance, security and adoption mechanisms are fully established.

**Security remains a phase-zero requirement.** Multi-factor authentication, monitoring and governance controls should be in place before any operational deployment due to the critical nature of the infrastructure and the presence of personal data.

## Why it is trustworthy

Every figure is grounded in the supplied datasets, assumptions are explicit and adjustable, limitations are clearly stated, and **privacy and security are integrated by design**.

The project applies data minimisation principles, avoids storing personal data in the source-code repository, relies on controlled access mechanisms and documents governance responsibilities.

The prototype is fully reproducible from the documentation and can serve as a foundation for future industrialisation.

## Bottom line

Néovolt Grid+ demonstrates the feasibility of a modern data platform capable of improving operational visibility, supporting consumption forecasting, strengthening security and enabling better decision-making.

We recommend that the steering committee approve a first implementation phase focused on the data platform, forecasting capabilities and governance framework, with security maintained as a mandatory deployment milestone.
